I've spent fifteen years walking into rooms where something was broken and nobody could name it. Scaling analytics from scratch at Booking.com. Launching digital governance at Danone that reached the boardroom. Building teams in places where no team existed.

Before the Fortune 500s: professional kitchens, military service, advertising, a consulting firm I co-founded, a startup I advised. Each chapter started the same way — with a mess, a gap, and the question: who's going to fix this?

I don't just talk about AI transformation. I build with it. I write code, deploy AI agents, and publish about what happens to people when the machines get faster than them. Most executives have an opinion about AI. I have a practice.

Based in Amsterdam. Bulgarian-born. Six languages. I write about the human side of technology at werkanders.com.
