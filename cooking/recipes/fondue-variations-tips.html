Hardware
Pan (Caquelon)
Better to use the original !! Ugly scene on the carpet if the handle of a cheap "Caquelon" brakes off !!!!
Preparation
Rub out the Clay pan with a half dove of garlic, rub really thoroughly! After that cut the garlic into pieces and throw it into the pot also.
Put white wine among recipe into the "Caquelon" and warm them slowly about 2 minutes. Not cooking the wine!
Give the cheese into the warm pan, not everything at once. Now a little more heat to melt the cheese but slowly, otherwise the fat comes out of the cheese and swims awful looking on top of the cheese sauce.
During the melting process you always have to stir up the cheese pulp. It doesn't matter in which direction, just slow and with a wooden tool to not damage the day pan.
Dissolve some cornstarch in a small glass filled with cherry Schnaps according to the recipe. If the cheese is completely melted then tip over the Schnaps -starch mix into the Caquelon. After this it's possible to put a tip on a knife from soda into the pulp, these makes the fondue more airy and more digestible. Be aware, the fondue starts to foam a little if you put in the soda, really just a very little bit of soda!!!!
Sample the fondue pepper and a little nutmeg
Tip
Shallot's or garlic are very tasty in a fondue. Up till 6 garlic doves, or 4 half shallot's are possible, it depends only at your table neighbours or your plans for the rest of the evening.
Troubleshooting
If the fondue is shifting during the preparation
Take the pot from the stove! Put a gulp of vinegar into the cheese and hit the pulp as hard as possible with an egg-beater. Maybe you save the patient before he's dead.
If the fondue is to liquid
Put some more cheese in, if You have Ieft.Otherwise put some more cornstarch into the melted cheese, but never as pure flour, always dissolve the starch first in some more schnaps or some water.
If the fondue is to viscid Agitate some more wine into the fondue, gulp by gulp.
The most important rule!
Most important rule to make a fondue is to feel like a real swiss person: Don't panik, take it easy and take some time for cooking and eating. During cooking process the fondue often does look to liquid or to viscid. Wait two minutes with some actions, after the cheese has complete melted.

SWISS CHEESE FONDUE (2-4 servings)

Ingredients:
0.5 garlic clove
150 g dry white wine
300 g Gruyère cheese or fondue mixture, grated
15 g cornstarch
White pepper (mill)
Nutmeg (pinch)
20 g Kirsch, Calvados, or Brandy
250 g white bread, cut into 2.5 cm cubes

Method:
1. Rub the inside of the fondue caquelon thoroughly with garlic clove; add cut garlic pieces to pot.
2. Pour wine into caquelon and warm slowly for 2 minutes—do not boil.
3. Add cheese gradually over gentle heat, stirring constantly with wooden spoon to prevent fat separation. Melt slowly until smooth.
4. Dissolve cornstarch in Kirsch, then add to melted cheese to thicken to creamy consistency. Optional: add a small pinch of baking soda for lighter texture.
5. Season with pepper mill and freshly ground nutmeg.
6. Place caquelon on rechaud and serve immediately with bread cubes.
7. Serve with chilled dry white wine or black tea.

Troubleshooting:
- If breaking/separating: remove from heat, add vinegar, beat vigorously with egg beater.
- If too liquid: add more cheese or dissolve additional cornstarch in Kirsch/water before adding.
- If too thick: add wine gradually, a splash at a time.

Key rule: Do not panic. Allow cheese to fully melt before adjusting consistency. Many fondue appear too liquid or thick during cooking—wait two minutes after melting completes.

Variations provided: Vacherin Mont-d'Or (baked 25 min at 170°C with wine), Bündner (three-cheese blend with butter-cornstarch base), Moitié-moitié (equal Vacherin Fribourgeois and Gruyère). For all: maintain steady temperature and stir while eating to prevent sticking.
