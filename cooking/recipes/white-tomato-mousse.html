WHITE TOMATO MOUSSE
Chef: Patrick Diethelm | DCT, Garde Manger | July 2005
Yield: 5 servings at 100g each

INGREDIENTS
600g fresh Italian ramata tomatoes
Salt and pepper to taste
Pinch of crystal sugar
10ml gin
1/4 lemon, juice
3 gelatin leaves
120ml heavy cream (36%)

METHOD
Wash very ripe tomatoes and cut into chunks. Add all remaining ingredients except cream and gelatin. Blend with bar blender to fine purée. Pour into cheesecloth and hang overnight in refrigerator to drain.

Line timbale or round molds with plastic foil. Soak gelatin leaves and dissolve with a little tomato liquid. Stir into remaining tomato liquid with gin, then season to taste with tabasco and salt. Whip heavy cream and fold in like bavarois once mousse begins to firm (22–26°C); do not fold in too early. Fill prepared molds and cool for 1 hour.

NOTE: For 250ml juice, use 3 gelatin leaves and 90–120g whipped cream, depending on desired texture.
