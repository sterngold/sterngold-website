PÂTÉ DOUGH
Source: DCT | Station: Garde Manger | Chef: Patrick Diethelm | Date: 28.11.2005
Yield: 0.900 kg

Ingredients:
- 504 g white wheat flour
- 174 g fresh butter
- 140 g water
- 11 g salt
- 60 g egg paste
- 12 g malt powder

Method:
In a pastry bowl, combine flour and cold butter, rubbing them together until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs. Make a well in the center.

Dissolve salt and malt powder in the water. Add egg paste and cold water to the well.

Combine ingredients and knead quickly into a dough. Do not overwork. Shape into a rectangle, cover, and refrigerate for 1 to 2 hours before use.

Note: The malt powder promotes even browning during baking. Work all ingredients together rapidly to prevent the dough from becoming crumbly or "burnt" as known in the trade.
