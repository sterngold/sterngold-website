FRENCH SAFFRON OYSTER SOUP WITH PERNOD
Yield: 6 servings

Ingredients:
12 fresh oysters (Creuse or Belon)
100 g dry white wine
600 g fish stock
200 g cream
10 g white roux
Saffron (pinch)
Salt and pepper
65 g young spinach leaves, blanched
30 g Pernod
30 g butter
20 g fennel brunoise
20 g carrot brunoise
20 g leek brunoise
50 g egg yolks
100 g cream (additional)
Garnish: fennel greens, melba toast

Method:
1. Wash and open oysters; reserve juice and oyster meats separately.
2. Blanch spinach and dry on paper towel.
3. Wrap each oyster in spinach leaf with small amount of egg white.
4. Combine Pernod, wine, fish stock, and reserved oyster water in rondeau.
5. Simmer and reduce to half volume; thicken with white roux and strain through china cap.
6. Add 200 g cream and saffron; bring to boil.
7. In sauteuse, sweat vegetable brunoise and add to soup.
8. Whisk egg yolks with 100 g cream. Temper liaison by slowly adding hot soup while whisking.
9. Add liaison to soup and stir over low heat; do not boil. Season to taste.
10. À la minute: warm fish stock to 65°C, add oysters briefly (few seconds).
11. Place one wrapped oyster in each espresso cup; ladle soup over.
12. Spoon remaining liaison on top and gratinate until golden.
13. Serve with melba toast and garnish with fennel greens.
