PIKE PERCH AND SALMON TERRINE
Chef Patrick Diethelm | DCT | 16.11.2005

Preparation: Line terrine mold carefully with plastic foil, then line with nori sheet. Skin pike perch fillets, dice, and refrigerate. Soak mu-err mushrooms in cold water. Peel bell peppers and cut into 5×5 mm jardinière. Chop fresh herbs (dill, chervil, parsley, tarragon).

Salmon garnish: Cut salmon into 2 long strips. Dredge salmon pieces in chopped herbs and season with salt and pepper.

Mousseline: Blend pike perch with egg whites, cream, and salt until smooth. Pass through drum sieve and place in bowl over ice. Season to taste with cayenne pepper, lemon juice, and melted honey. Fold in bell peppers, pistachios, and mu-err mushrooms.

Assembly: Fill terrine mold with approximately 1/4 of fish mousseline farce. Place first salmon strip in center and press down. Fill with additional farce, place second salmon fillet on top, and cover with remaining farce. Ensure no air bubbles remain. Cover with overlapping nori sheet and plastic foil.

Cooking: Poach in convection oven at 70–75°C until center reaches 55–58°C. Weigh down slightly and cool in mold.
