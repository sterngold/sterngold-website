Belgian Endive

Ingredients for 2 servings at 100% (Net quantity)
300 g Belgian endive
2 g Salt
10 g Butter
40 g White Matignon
1 x Seasoning (salt and pepper)
60 ml *Vegetable Stock
0.5 pc Lemon 10 g Butter
Wash endives, trim if necessary. With a pointed knife, remove the bitter core. Be sure endive leaves do not separate. Blanch endives in salt and lemon water, drain.

- ADD BUTTER IN SUITABLE RONDEAU PAN.
- ADD MATIGNON AND SWEAT.
- PLACE ENDIVE TIGHTLY, SIDE BY SIDE, ON TOP.
- ADD VEGETABLE STOCK AND LEMON JUICE.
THE STOCK SHOULD COVER ABOUT ONE THIRD OF THE VEGETABLES. -SEASON WITH SALT AND PEPPER. COVER WITH BUTTERED ALUMINUM FOIL -BRING TO A BOIL.
- BRAISE IN OVEN AT 180°C UNTIL BELGIAN ENDIVE ARE TENDER. - COOL WWN WITH STOCK IN ICE WATER
- REMOVE FROM STOCK
- CUT LENGHTWISE IN HALF OR QUARTER AND TRIM
- SEASON WITH SALT AND PEPPER
- SAUTE IN CLARIFIED BUTTER UNTIL GOLDEN BROWN AND SERVE - BRUSH WITH HOT BUTTER AND SERVE.
Up to date methods for serving

Belgian endive can also be breaded and then sauteed in butter until golden brown with some herbs
or Parisian style or Piccata style
Wash 300g Belgian endives and trim if necessary. Using a pointed knife, remove the bitter core while keeping leaves intact. Blanch in salted lemon water and drain.

Melt butter in a suitable rondeau pan. Add matignon (white vegetable dice) and sweat. Arrange blanched endives tightly, side by side, on top. Add vegetable stock and lemon juice—stock should cover about one-third of the vegetables. Season with salt and pepper. Cover with buttered aluminum foil and bring to a boil. Braise in oven at 180°C until tender. Cool with stock in ice water. Remove from stock, cut lengthwise in half or quarter, and trim. Season with salt and pepper. Sauté in clarified butter until golden brown. Brush with hot butter and serve.

Alternatively, bread the endives and sauté in butter until golden, or prepare Parisian or Piccata style.
