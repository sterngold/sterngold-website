SWISS CHEESE FONDUES VARIATIONS

--- FONDUE VACHERIN MONT-D'OR ---
Yield: 4 servings | Time: 25 minutes

Ingredients:
1 Vacherin Mont-d'Or (with wooden box)
1 loaf semi-white bread, cubed
1 small glass white wine

Method:
1. Wrap aluminum foil around wooden box containing Vacherin (do not cover cheese top).
2. Use fork to puncture the Vacherin crust.
3. Pour white wine over cheese.
4. Bake at 170°C for 25 minutes.

--- BÜNDNER CHEESE FONDUE ---
Yield: 4 servings

Ingredients:
800 g Bündnerkäse (mix of Brigelser, Nufener, and Münstertaler)
30 g cornstarch
3 dl white wine
Pepper and nutmeg
Butter

Method:
1. Mix one level teaspoon cornstarch with slightly melted butter in fondue pot.
2. Thin with 3 dl white wine and stir constantly.
3. Add cheese and stir until melted.
4. Season with pepper and nutmeg to taste.
5. Maintain on low heat.

--- FONDUE MOITIÉ-MOITIÉ (Half and Half) ---
Yield: 4 servings

Ingredients:
600-800 g French bread, cut into 2-3 cm cubes
1 garlic clove, peeled and crushed
0.3 l dry wine
45 g cornstarch
400 g Vacherin Fribourgeois
400 g Gruyère, grated
Pepper and Kirsch to taste

Method:
1. Rub caquelon with crushed garlic.
2. Mix cornstarch into caquelon and bring wine to boil.
3. Add both cheeses and melt over very low heat, stirring continuously with spatula.
4. Add pepper and Kirsch to taste.
5. Place caquelon on chafing dish once fondue reaches creamy consistency.
6. Maintain at steady temperature throughout service.
7. Spear bread cubes on fork and dip into fondue. Stir well while eating to prevent sticking.
