**GNOCCHI MALFATTI—ITALIAN SPINACH DUMPLINGS**

Yield: 5 servings

**Ingredients:**
- 200 g fresh spinach (or 70 g frozen blanched)
- 30 g shallots, finely chopped
- 1 clove garlic, crushed
- 20 g unsalted butter
- 75 g ricotta cheese
- 50 g pasteurized eggs
- 70 g white wheat flour
- 15 g buckwheat flour
- 60 g freshly grated parmesan
- Salt, pepper, nutmeg to taste
- 2 g chopped basil and marjoram

**Garnish:** 25 g butter, 15 g chopped basil, grated parmesan, 150 g tomato coulis

**Method:**
1. Wash spinach thoroughly in cold water. Remove stalks. Blanch briefly and refresh. Squeeze completely dry. Chop finely or blend with eggs.
2. Sweat shallots and garlic in butter 1-2 minutes. Cool.
3. Combine all ingredients. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg. Rest 20 minutes.
4. Shape into quenelle-form dumplings using two spoons.
5. Simmer in large pot of lightly salted boiling water. Cook 2-4 minutes after dumplings float to surface. Drain carefully.
6. Heat fresh sage roughly chopped in hazelnut butter. Pour over hot gnocchi.
7. Plate on tomato coulis. Finish with freshly grated parmesan.
