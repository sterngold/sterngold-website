STUFFED ONIONS WITH VEGETABLES

Yield: 6 servings
Chef: P. Diethelm | Cost: CHF 0.51/EUR 0.32 per serving

INGREDIENTS
300g Onions (3 medium, same size)
80ml Beef broth
20g Sbrinz, grated
40g Butter
60g Smoked bacon, sliced
70g Vegetables, jardinière
60g Tomato concasse
4g Italian parsley, fresh, chopped
50g White bread (Pullman loaf)
1g Marjoram, fresh or basil
Salt and pepper to taste

METHOD
1. Peel onions, cut off tops, and leave root ends intact. Blanch briefly in salted water or steam lightly.
2. Carefully scoop out centers to form cups, maintaining structure at the root. Cups should be at least 2 layers thick. Reserve chopped scooped onion.
3. Cut bacon into small brunoise; cut seasonal vegetables into jardinière; prepare tomato concasse; chop parsley and marjoram; remove bread crust and cut into small croutons, then toast under salamander until golden.
4. Sauté bacon in butter, add vegetables. Fold in parsley, marjoram, croutons, reserved chopped onions, and concasse. Season to taste.
5. Arrange onion cups in a gratin platter. Fill with stuffing and pour bouillon around onions. Cover with buttered aluminum foil.
6. Bake at 165°C until onions are brown. Remove foil, sprinkle with cheese, and brown in hot oven or under salamander.
