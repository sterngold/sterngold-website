RECIPE FOR TWO SMALLER PATÉ MOLDS
PÂTÉ DE GIBIER EN CROÛTE
Source: DCT | Station: Garde Manger | Chef: Patrick Diethelm | Date: 28.11.2005
Yield: 10 servings

Farce Ingredients:
- 75 g venison meat, boneless and trimmed
- 75 g veal shoulder, trimmed and boneless
- 60 g fatback
- 30 g calf's liver
- 500 mg juniper berries
- 40 ml Madeira wine
- 15 ml Calvados
- 10 g shallots
- 40 g apples
- 30 g glace de viande
- 75 ml heavy cream (36%)
- 3 g sea salt
- 250 mg pâté spice
- 5 g horn of plenty mushrooms, soaked
- 8 g ham, 5 mm dice
- 3 g pumpkin seeds
- 3 g sunflower seeds
- 5 g pistachios
- 75 g venison tenderloin for garnish
- 25 g fatback for garnish
- 3 g clarified butter
- 225 g pâté dough

Method:
Marinate venison meat chunks with juniper berries, rosemary, thyme, Calvados, and Madeira. Prepare fatback and liver in chunks. Peel, core, and slice apples.

Separate and drain the marinated meat, reducing the marinade with meat glaze; keep warm. Sauté venison and veal in clarified butter; keep warm. Sauté apples and shallots (emincé) and combine with meat. Add fatback to the meat mixture.

Grind hot meat mixture in food processor, adding raw liver and fatback last. Blend in two-thirds of the cream and meat glaze until smooth. Season to taste. Fold in sautéed mushrooms, roasted nuts, and ham or tongue.

Line a pâté mold with dough. Fill one-third with farce. Place prepared venison tenderloin in center, cover with remaining farce. Brush dough rim with egg wash-cream mixture. Cover with dough, create chimneys for steam escape, decorate top with dough pieces. Refrigerate 1 hour.

Brush with egg wash. Bake at 250°C for 10 minutes. Open chimney holes and continue baking at 180°C with vent open until internal temperature reaches 55°C. Cool to 25°C, then pour hot aspic jelly through chimneys. Refrigerate until completely set.
