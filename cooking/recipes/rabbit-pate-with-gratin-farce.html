**Rabbit Pâté with Gratin Farce**
*Chef: Patrick Diethelm | Recipe #269 | 8 servings*

**Farce Preparation:**
Marinate rabbit, veal, and liver with juniper berries, orange zest, Port wine, and Calvados. Separate meats and reduce marinade with glaze. Keep warm.

Quickly sauté cold rabbit and veal in clarified butter. Spread on sheet pan, season lightly with salt and pepper. Sauté emince shallots and add to meat. Add fatback strips.

Grind hot meat mixture while still warm with raw liver and remaining fatback. Transfer to food processor and blend until smooth mousseline consistency with 2/3 of the cream and glaze. Season to taste. Stir in remaining ingredients (apricots, seeds, truffles, pistachios).

**Assembly:**
Line pâté mold with fatback slices. Fill mold approximately 1/3 high with farce, ensuring no air bubbles. Place prepared rabbit loins in center, cover with remaining farce. Brush dough rim with egg yolk–cream mixture. Cover with dough, create steam vents (chimneys), decorate with dough pieces, and brush with egg yolk–cream mixture. Chill 1 hour.

Bake at 250°C for 10 minutes. Open chimney vents, continue baking at 180°C until center reaches 58°C. Cool to 25°C. Pour cool aspic through chimney holes and refrigerate until set.
