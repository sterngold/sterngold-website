Austrian Napkin Dumpling

Chef: P.Diethelm Source: DCT

Production

VITZNAU-LUCERNE SWITZERLAND
AUSTRIAN NAPKIN DUMPLING WITH HERBS
Yield: 4 servings
150 g white roll bread without crust, cut into small 3mm dices
20 g white wheat flour 70 ml cold milk
30 g fine chopped shallots
6 g fresh plugged marjoram leaves
8 g fine chopped parsley
110g butter
2 g salt
pinch of nutmeg
60 g egg
Preparation:
- Dry the white bread cubes at 190° C until golden brown
- Mix the '150 g croutons with the flour - Pour in the cold milk and stir carefully - Allow to rest for 15 min.
- Sweat the chopped shallots with 10 g butter on low heat, add the marjoram and
fine chopped parsley and mix with the bread
- Whip the remaining butter foamy, add the egg, salt, nutmeg and keep on whipping
- Add in the bread mixture and mix carefully - Knead lightly and allow to rest for 1 hour
- Form a roll 6 cm diameter and roll into a napkin or cheese cloth
- Tight up the ends with butchers twine
- Poach for about 20 min. in lightly salted water
- Remove from the water and cool down, but keep warm
- Remove the napkin and slice into 1,5 cm thick slices
Served with braised or glazed meat dishes
P. Diethelm 05 - EGC
Toast bread cubes at 190°C until golden brown. Mix 150 g croutons with flour, pour in cold milk, and rest 15 minutes. Sweat chopped shallots in 10 g butter on low heat; add marjoram and parsley, combine with bread mixture. Whip remaining butter to foam, add egg, salt, and nutmeg; continue whipping. Add bread mixture and fold carefully. Knead lightly and rest 1 hour. Form a 6 cm diameter roll, wrap in napkin or cheesecloth, and tie ends with butcher's twine. Poach in lightly salted water for approximately 20 minutes. Remove and cool while keeping warm. Unwrap and slice into 1.5 cm thick slices. Serve with braised or glazed meat dishes.
