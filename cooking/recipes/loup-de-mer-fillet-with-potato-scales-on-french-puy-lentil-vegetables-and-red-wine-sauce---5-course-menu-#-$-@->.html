VITZNAU-LUCERNE SWITZERLAND
LOUP DE MER FILLET WITH POTATO SCALES ON FRENCH PUY LENTIL VEGETABLES AND RED WINE SAUCE

Yield: 4 servings
4 Loup de mer fillets [see coure instruction) salt, pepper
white wheat flour
some cream
2 potatoes B type
1 egg white clarified butter
100 g leek julienne 8 cm long
peanut oil for frying
Lentil vegetable:
40 g small brown lentils [soaked 1 hour in vegetable stock) French puy lentils
1 sprigs of thyme
¼ bay leaf
olive oil
20 g bacon brunoise
40 g vegetable brunoise


Red wine sauce
20 g shallot émincé
150 g red wine
50 g Port wine
100 g brown veal stock [reduced)
30 g olive oil

Preparation:
- Boil the lentils, thyme and bay leaf in the vegetable stock until well done and drain them - Sweat the bacon in olive oil, add the vegetable brunoise and mix with cooked lentils - Dredge fish fillets in wheat flour and brush with some cream
- Slice the peeled potatoes with the mandoline in very thin slices and steam quickly
add the hot potato slices in egg white and place on the fish fillet as a tiled roof
- Chill until service

- For the sauce, sweat fine chopped shallots in butter, pour in red wine, port wine and veal stock and reduce to desired consistency
- Strain through china cap
- Stir in the olive oil on low heat and cover

- Season and saute the fish fillet carefully, first at potato side down into the hot butter,
- Fry the leek julienne crispy, and keep warm
- Spread the lentil vegetables on the hot plate, arrange the fish fillet and pour some sauce around the dish
- Place the fryed leek julienne
- Garnish with sprigs of herbs.
