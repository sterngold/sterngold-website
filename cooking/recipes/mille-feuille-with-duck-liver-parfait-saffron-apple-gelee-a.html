MILLE FEUILLE WITH DUCK LIVER PARFAIT, SAFFRON APPLE GELEE AND HAZELNUT PUFF PASTRY

Critical, terrine mold
Yield: 10 servings
150 g Duck liver
200 g beurre noisette
30 g egg
20 g egg yolk
50 g Madeira
25 g glaze de viande
5 g salt,
pepper,nutmeg
1 sprig of thyme
1 sprig of rosemary

Preparation:
Soak the liver for about 30 minutes with cold milk.
Rinse, clean and dry on kitchen paper
Reduce madeira, port wine, glace and herbs to 1/3 and strain, add to liver
Cook the butter to a light brown hazelnut butter and strain through a fine napkin.
Cool down until luke warm.
Add the liver, wine reduction and eggs into the blender.
Pour in the luke warm butter in a steady stream.
Seasone to taste with salt, and nutmeg
Strain throug fine sieve and fill into prepared silpat mold or small plastic terrine molds line out with plastic foil
Cover with plastic foil
Poach at 80 C. until well done.
Cool down and let rest in the refrigerator for 1 hour


APPLE GELEE
200 g apple peeled, emincé
10 g lemon juice
20 g sugar
50 g white wine, pinch of saffron
4 g sheets of gelatine

50 g ground hazelnuts
150 g puff pastry – roll out puff pastry 1.5 mm – blind bake


Preparation:
Stew the sliced apples with lemon juice, sugar, saffron and white wine until cooked.
Strain through a hair sieve or food mill.
Stir in the soaked and squeezed - out gelatine and cool down.
Spread the compote on the cold liver parfait

Slice the blind baked hazelnut pastry into mold size and press on the apple gelee layer.

Let rest on cold place for 30 minutes.
Unmold the mille feuille and serve

Garnish with crispy dried apple rings and drizzle with Raspberry balsamic coulis.
and serve with small winter herb salad

Crispy apple décor:
Cut the whole green apple carefuly into 1 mm thin slices.
Blanch them quickly in 50m1 lemon juice or little ascorbin acid and 1 dl water.
Refresh and dry at 80°C. oven temp. with open steam escape on silpat mat until crispy
