Gnocchi Parisian Style

Chef: P.Diethelm Source: DCT

Production

Gnocchi Parisian Style
Brandteignocken Gnocchi parisienne


Rezept Nr. 627
Kategorie : Pasta Dishes
Koch : Patrick Diethelm
Datum : 16.08.2005
Warenkosten : CHF 0.53 / EUR 0.34

EUROPEAN CULINARY CENTER

Zutaten fir 9 Personen zu 100% (Nettomenge)


203 ml Milk
72 g Butter
5 g Salt
1 x Nutmeg (pinch)
210 g White wheat flour
225 g Egg Shelled & broken up
18 g Butter
300 ml *Cream Sauce (with cream)
90 g Sbrinz grated
54 g Butter melted


- Combine milk, butter, salt, and nutmeg and bring to a boil.
Remove from heat and add sifted flour all at once.
Return to heat and stir vigorously with a plastic spatula until the resulting stiff paste no longer clings to the bottom of the pot.
Cool mixture slightly; stir in eggs in small increments.


BRING AMPLE AMOUNT OF WATER TO SIMMER.
PUT PUFF PASTE IN PASTRY BAG WITH A LARGE, SMOOTH TIP. PIPE DUMPLINGS DIRECTLY INTO THE WATER 25 mm TO MAKE DUMPLINGS, OR USE TWO TABLE SPOONS
REMOVE DUMPLINGS WITH SKIMMER AS SOON AS THEY FLOAT TO THE TOP, AND PUT THEM INTO COLD WATER.
WHEN ALL PASTE IS USED UP, LIFT DUMPLINGS FROM COLD WATER AND LET THEM DRAIN OFF.
- BUTTER A DEEP, OVENPROOF DISH (GRATIN DISH).
BRING CREAM SAUCE TO A BOIL.
- SPOON A SMALL AMOUNT OF CREAM SAUCE INTO THE GRATIN DISH AND PLACE DUMPLINGS ON TOP. DO NOT CROWD DUMPLINGS; THEY WILL EXPAND DURING BAKING.
COVER WITH REMAINING CREAM SAUCE AND SPRINKLE WITH CHEESE AND MELTED BUTTER.
BAKE FOR 10-20 MINUTES, STARTING AT 180°C, AND FINISHING AT 220°C .
Note: The dumplings should be golden brown, and should have risen like a soufflé. If not served at once, they will collapse.
PANADE: Combine milk, butter, salt, and a pinch of nutmeg and bring to a boil. Remove from heat and add sifted flour all at once. Return to heat and stir vigorously with a plastic spatula until the paste no longer clings to the pot bottom. Cool slightly, then stir in eggs in small increments until fully incorporated.

COOKING: Bring an ample amount of water to a simmer. Transfer paste to a pastry bag fitted with a large smooth tip and pipe dumplings directly into the water, 25 mm in size. Alternatively, use two tablespoons. Remove dumplings with a skimmer as soon as they float to the surface and transfer to cold water. Once all paste is used, lift dumplings from cold water and drain.

ASSEMBLY & BAKING: Butter a deep ovenproof gratin dish. Bring cream sauce to a boil. Spoon a small amount of sauce into the gratin dish, then arrange dumplings on top without crowding—they will expand during baking. Cover with remaining sauce and sprinkle with grated Sbrinz and melted butter. Bake at 180°C for 10–20 minutes, finishing at 220°C until golden brown and risen like a soufflé. Serve immediately; the dish will collapse if held.
