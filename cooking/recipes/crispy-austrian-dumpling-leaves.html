**Crispy Austrian Dumpling Leaves** | Chef: Patrick Diethelm | 12.12.2005

Cut bacon into very fine brunoise. Finely chop shallots and dice bread into 1 cm × 1 cm cubes. Chop parsley.

Sauté shallots and bacon in 20g butter, then add chopped herbs and transfer to bowl. Toast diced bread with remaining 80g butter in 180°C oven, then add to shallots and bacon mixture. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg.

Bring milk to boil and add to bowl without stirring. Cool completely. Fold in eggs and breadcrumbs (mie de pain). Roll mixture tightly in plastic foil and poach in 85–90°C salted water for 1 hour. Remove and cool.

Slice on meat slicer into 2–3 mm thick slices. Place on parchment and dry in 80°C oven for 20 minutes, or leave overnight in plate warmer to crisp. Serve as garnish for amuse-bouche or appetizers.
